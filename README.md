**Retail Customer Behavior Analysis**

**Project Overview**
This project focuses on analyzing customer shopping behavior to identify key patterns in purchasing, customer segmentation, and product performance. The goal is to derive actionable insights that can help improve business decision-making, customer retention, and revenue growth.

**Business Problem** 
Retail businesses often lack clear visibility into:
Who their most valuable customers are
Which products drive the most revenue
How customer behavior varies across segments
This project aims to address these gaps using data analysis.

**Tools & Technologies**
SQL – Data extraction and aggregation
Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib/Seaborn) – Data cleaning and exploratory analysis
Power BI – Interactive dashboard and visualization

**Project Workflow**
Data Collection
Used customer shopping dataset (.csv)
Data Extraction (SQL)
Queried data to calculate:
Total revenue
Top customers
Sales by category
Data Cleaning & Analysis (Python)
Handled missing values and data inconsistencies
Performed exploratory data analysis (EDA)
Identified trends and patterns
Data Visualization (Power BI)
Built an interactive dashboard to present:
Customer segmentation
Revenue trends
Category performance

**Dashboard Highlights**
Total Customers: 3.9K
Average Purchase Value: $59.76
Average Rating: 3.75
Revenue and sales breakdown by category
Customer distribution by subscription status
Sales and revenue by age group

**Key Insights**
~73% of customers are non-subscribers
A small group of customers contributes a large portion of revenue
Young adult segment shows the highest sales contribution
Certain product categories outperform others significantly


 **Business Recommendations**
Target high-value customers with personalized offers
Improve subscription conversion strategies
Focus marketing efforts on high-performing categories
Optimize inventory based on demand trends
